The Power of Building Community

Beaver Meadow: More Than a Golf Course — A Year-Round Destination

Beaver Meadow is more than just a summer destination. With the right infrastructure — including a functioning, modern clubhouse — it has the potential to become a four-season hub for recreation. Plans for expanded outdoor winter activities like cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, and skating are on the table. But we cannot truly begin to grow these offerings without investing in a year-round facility that can serve as a home base for all users.

Without a clubhouse, we’re not just limiting golfers — we’re turning away winter families, school groups, and community events that would otherwise thrive here. And how can we call ourselves a welcoming community if we alienate our neighbors?

Clearing Up the Misinformation

There’s a false narrative circulating that non-residents are unfairly subsidized at Beaver Meadow — that they pay the same greens fees as Concord residents. That simply isn’t true. The rate structure changed more than a year ago to reflect a fair and thoughtful balance between encouraging regional participation and respecting Concord taxpayers. Non-resident rates were adjusted to ensure that all who enjoy Beaver Meadow contribute accordingly.

Beaver Meadow is owned by the people of Concord, but it serves a much wider audience. And that’s something to be proud of — not something to gatekeep.

In Support of Our Shared Future

Supporting Beaver Meadow — and supporting public and community-oriented golf throughout central New Hampshire — means investing in more than recreation. It means protecting spaces that bring people together, support local economies, and help towns stay connected to both tradition and each other.

Whether you're a regular on the tee sheet or someone who's never picked up a club, there's a place for you at Beaver Meadow — and at every course in our region that puts community first. With the right investment, we can turn Beaver Meadow into a year-round destination that serves everyone — golfers, skiers, skaters, neighbors, and friends.
